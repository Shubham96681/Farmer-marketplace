# Feasibility Report: Farm-to-Table Produce App

## 1. Introduction
The Farm-to-Table Produce App connects local farmers directly with consumers and restaurants, reducing middlemen, lowering costs, and increasing farmer profit.

## 2. Market Feasibility
- **Target Users:** Farmers, households, small restaurants.
- **Market Size:** Agriculture contributes ~25% to Nigeria’s GDP; large demand for fresh produce.
- **Problem:** Farmers struggle with poor logistics and unfair pricing.
- **Opportunity:** Digitized platform bridges gap between farmers and buyers.

## 3. Technical Feasibility
- **Frontend:** HTML, CSS, Vanilla JS.
- **Backend:** FastAPI (Python).
- **Database:** PostgreSQL.
- **Deployment:** AWS EC2 with GitHub Actions CI/CD.
- **Payment Integration:** Flutterwave or Paystack.
- **Logistics:** Integration with local delivery services (future phase).

## 4. Financial Feasibility
- **Revenue Streams:**
  - Commission (5–10%) per transaction.
  - Delivery fee margin.
  - Premium listings for farmers.
- **Startup Costs:** Hosting (AWS), domain name, developer time.
- **ROI:** Profitable when scaling to 500+ farmers and 5,000+ buyers.

## 5. Operational Feasibility
- Farmers onboard via simple registration.
- Buyers browse and order online.
- Admins monitor transactions and verify farmers.
- Local delivery partners handle last-mile logistics.

## 6. Risk Analysis
- **Risk:** Low digital literacy among farmers.
  - *Mitigation:* Offer training and USSD support in future.
- **Risk:** Competition from existing e-commerce platforms.
  - *Mitigation:* Niche focus on agriculture + transparency.
- **Risk:** Payment failures or fraud.
  - *Mitigation:* Use trusted payment gateways.

## 7. Conclusion
The project is technically and financially feasible with high potential impact in Nigeria’s agricultural sector.
